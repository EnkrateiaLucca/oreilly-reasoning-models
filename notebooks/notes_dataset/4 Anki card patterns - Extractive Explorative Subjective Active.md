Four patterns I see in the Anki cards I make. Each maps to a different cognitive job:

- **Extractive** — pulls a specific fact, definition, or quote out of source material. Anchored by a Source URL/quote.
- **Explorative** — opens a question or invites a connection ("How does X relate to Y?"). Designed to make you think, not just recall.
- **Subjective** — captures your own opinion, preference, or interpretation. Useful for consistency of self over time and for surfacing your taste.
- **Active** — primes a behavior or attentional pattern ("When I'm reading X, look for Y"). See [[Anki cards as priming not task management]].

Most cards default to Extractive. Deliberately mixing the other three is where Anki gets interesting — it turns the deck into a tool for shaping cognition, not just storing facts.
