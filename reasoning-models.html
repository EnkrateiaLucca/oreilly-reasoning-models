What are Reasoning Models?

Large language models can be prompted to show their reasoning process, leading to dramatic improvements in problem-solving capabilities.

üîó Chain-of-Thought

Generate intermediate reasoning steps before arriving at an answer, just like solving a math problem step-by-step.

Key benefit: Emergent reasoning in large models through simple prompting

üå≥ Tree of Thoughts

Explore multiple reasoning paths as a tree structure, with the ability to backtrack and evaluate different branches.

Key benefit: 74% success on Game of 24 (vs 4% with standard prompting)

üï∏Ô∏è Graph of Thoughts

Extend beyond trees to graphs where thoughts can have complex dependencies and feedback loops.

Key benefit: 62% better sorting quality while reducing costs by 31%

üîÑ Self-Consistency

Generate multiple diverse reasoning paths and select the most consistent answer via majority voting.

Key benefit: Up to 18% accuracy improvement on math problems

ü§ñ ReAct Framework

Combine reasoning with actions, allowing models to interact with external tools and information sources.

Key benefit: Grounds reasoning in reality, reduces hallucination

üìö Least-to-Most

Decompose complex problems into simpler subproblems, solving them sequentially from easiest to hardest.

Key benefit: Enables solving problems harder than training examples

Evolution of Reasoning Methods

A journey through the development of LLM reasoning techniques

2022

Chain-of-Thought Emerges

Wei et al. discover that large language models can perform complex reasoning by generating intermediate steps. Simple prompting with examples leads to dramatic improvements on math and logic problems.

2022

Self-Consistency & Least-to-Most

Wang et al. introduce majority voting across multiple reasoning paths. Zhou et al. show that decomposition enables easy-to-hard generalization.

2023

Tree of Thoughts & ReAct

Yao et al. introduce deliberate tree search for reasoning. ReAct combines reasoning with external actions, grounding LLMs in reality.

2023

Step-Back Prompting

Zheng et al. show that abstracting to first principles before solving improves performance on scientific reasoning tasks.

2024

Graph of Thoughts

Extension beyond trees to arbitrary graph structures enables complex thought dependencies and feedback loops.

2025

RL-Based Reasoning

DeepSeek-R1 demonstrates that reasoning capabilities can emerge from pure reinforcement learning, representing a paradigm shift in training approaches.
